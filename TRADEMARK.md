Version 1.0 - March 2026

## Overview

TeamHero and Kapow are open source software projects released under the MIT License. The MIT License gives you broad rights to use, modify, and distribute the **code**. However, this license does not grant any rights to use the **TeamHero** or **Kapow** trademarks, logos, or branding.

This trademark policy exists to protect users and the community from confusion about the origin of products and services, while keeping things as open and permissive as possible.

## Permitted Uses (No Permission Needed)

You do NOT need permission to:

- **Refer to TeamHero or Kapow by name** in articles, blog posts, talks, presentations, podcasts, videos, academic papers, or documentation.
- **Describe compatibility or integration.** For example:
  - "Built with TeamHero"
  - "Powered by TeamHero"
  - "Compatible with TeamHero"
  - "Works with Kapow"
  - "Integrates with TeamHero"
- **Use the name in community discussions,** forums, tutorials, how-to guides, and educational materials.
- **State factual information** about your product's relationship with TeamHero or Kapow, such as "Our service supports TeamHero deployments."
- **Use the name in package/module names** that are clearly utilities or plugins, such as `teamhero-docker-compose` or `kapow-theme-dark`, provided it is clear these are community projects and not official releases.

### Attribution Requirement

When referencing TeamHero or Kapow in published materials, include this notice where practical:

> TeamHero and Kapow are trademarks of Sagi Yaacoby.

## Not Permitted

The following uses are **not permitted** and will not be approved:

### Product and Business Names
- You may NOT use "TeamHero" or "Kapow" (or any confusingly similar variation such as "Team-Hero," "MyTeamHero," "KapowAI," "TeamHeroPlus") as part of:
  - Your product name
  - Your service name
  - Your company name or trade name
  - Your application name in app stores

### Domain Names
- You may NOT register domain names containing "TeamHero" or "Kapow" (e.g., teamhero-hosting.com, kapow-cloud.io, getteamhero.com).

### Logo Misuse
- You may NOT modify, distort, recolor, or combine the TeamHero or Kapow logos with other marks or imagery.

## Forked or Modified Versions

If you fork or create a modified version of TeamHero or Kapow, the MIT License gives you every right to do so. However:

1. **You MUST rename your fork.** Your forked or modified version cannot use "TeamHero," "Kapow," or any confusingly similar name (e.g., "TeamHero Plus," "Kapow Pro," "OpenTeamHero").
2. **You MUST remove all TeamHero and Kapow logos and branding** from your fork, including icons, splash screens, and marketing materials.
3. **You MUST include a clear notice** in your fork's README or equivalent documentation:

   > This project is an independent fork and is not affiliated with, endorsed by, or connected to TeamHero, Kapow, or Sagi Yaacoby.

4. You are free to state that your project is "based on TeamHero" or "derived from TeamHero" as a factual description of its origin.

These rules follow the same approach used by WordPress, Docker, Node.js, and Rust - the code is free, but the name stays with the project.

## Enforcement

We believe in working with the community, not against it.

- **First contact:** If we find a potential trademark violation, we will reach out with a polite request to correct the issue. Most cases are honest mistakes and are resolved quickly.
- **Escalation:** Legal action is a last resort, used only when a party refuses to cooperate after reasonable attempts to resolve the matter.
- **Good faith:** We will never take action against fair use, honest references, community projects, or constructive criticism.

If you are unsure whether your intended use is permitted, please ask. We would rather help you get it right than enforce after the fact.

## Why This Policy Exists

Open source licenses like MIT cover copyright - they let you use, modify, and share the code. But they do not cover trademarks. Without a trademark policy:

- Anyone could release software called "TeamHero" that has nothing to do with this project.
- Users could be confused about which version is official and which is a fork.
- Bad actors could trade on the project's reputation.

This policy protects the community by ensuring that when someone sees "TeamHero" or "Kapow," they know what they are getting.
